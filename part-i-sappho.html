part i: disciples of sappho

introduction, the isle of lesbos

for well over two thousand years, the influence of sappho on lesbian and queer history are undeniable. Her name and home on lesbos inspire the very language we use to describe women who love women. and yet, we can be sure of almost nothing about the woman herself. only fragments of her work survive and we've relied on descriptions of her life created by those who came after her.

alcaeus of mytilene is a potential contemporary of hers. his works are also fragmented but include a reference to sappho that may be interpreted as having known her. but other than this scrap, we won’t hear of her again on the written page until plato’s phaedrus, two hundred years later. although the thread of sappho's disciples is often lost for decades and centuries at a time it always comes back around. most recently, sappho has been inspiring movements to write queer people back onto history’s pages.

what impression can made of sappho by peering through all the different interpretations of her poetry, the anthropological sleuthing of her life, and the legends of her lovers as seen through the artists she’s inspired? ultimately, whatever can be pieced together during this read through history will be a fiction. my hope is simply to explore sappho's influence on queer literary history.

anne carson
